# Desire-Based Hook Templates

5 proven templates for writing hooks that convert views into leads and sales. Each template combines a **dream outcome** with a **relatable character** under **constraint-free conditions**.

---

## Template 1: About Me

**Character:** Creator (you) | **Direction:** Looking backward (already achieved)

**Mad Lib:** `I just accomplished [X dream outcome] using [Y relatable method]`

Optionally add a second sentence emphasizing the method is easy/constraint-free.

### Examples

**Example 1 — Social media growth niche:**
> "I took a break from posting for almost a month and still grew by 11,000 followers. And this is why."

- Dream outcome: Growing followers without doing any work
- Character: Creator (Sammy) — normal, relatable, no intimidating advantages
- Constraint-free: "by literally doing nothing" → conditions = zero
- Result: 2,000 leads

**Example 2 — Instagram sales niche:**
> "I took my Instagram story views from this to this, and it took my sales from this to this. I don't care what niche you're in. If you use this one story strategy, I promise that you will increase your views and your sales."

- Dream outcome: Growing views AND sales dramatically
- Character: Creator (Cassie) — super relatable, looks like anyone could do it
- Constraint-free: "one story strategy" → just one simple tactic
- Result: 1,600 leads

## Template 2: If I

**Character:** Creator (you) | **Direction:** Looking forward (hypothetical scenario)

**Mad Lib:** `If I wanted to achieve [X outcome], I would use [Y method]`

Optionally add a second sentence explaining the relatable/easy approach.

### Examples

**Example 1 — Social media growth:**
> "Pretty confident that I could rebuild this account back to where it is right now in less than 6 months. Not by doing anything crazy, but by doing this one thing from day one."

- Dream outcome: Big social following (175K) in short time (6 months)
- Character: Sammy — normal house background, nothing intimidating
- Constraint-free: "not by doing anything crazy, just one thing"
- Result: 1,000 leads

**Example 2 — Content monetization:**
> "If I were starting from scratch, here's exactly what I would do to turn my content into cash flow in three simple steps."

- Dream outcome: Turning content into cash from zero
- Character: Cassie — high-quality content but seems like "you and me"
- Constraint-free: "three simple steps" — anyone can do it
- Result: 3,300 leads from one 45-second video

## Template 3: To You

**Character:** Viewer (you the audience) | **Direction:** Statement about them

**Mad Lib:** `If you're trying to do [X outcome], use [Y relatable method]`

The character is the viewer themselves — doesn't matter if they find the creator relatable.

### Examples

**Example 1 — AI tools niche:**
> "If you want to get better at using AI, this one simple trick will make your life so much easier."

- Dream outcome: Getting better at AI
- Character: The viewer ("you") — self-identifies, no creator judgment needed
- Constraint-free: "one simple trick"
- Result: 21,000 leads (42,000 comments, half converted)

## Template 4: Can You

**Character:** Viewer | **Direction:** Hypothetical question

**Mad Lib:** `Is it possible for you to achieve [X outcome] under [Y relatable conditions]?`

Framed as a question instead of a statement — creates engagement and curiosity.

### Examples

**Example 1 — Business/entrepreneurship:**
> "Is it possible to start a business in one day and make $1,000 from it?"

- Dream outcome: Starting a profitable business in one day
- Character: Creator (Minnow Lee) "seems just like a kid, waking up in his bed"
- Constraint-free: "in one day" — makes it feel fast and accessible
- Result: 5,000-6,000 leads per video, ran format 8 times → 24M views total

## Template 5: He/She Just Did

**Character:** Third party (case study) | **Direction:** Someone else's achievement

**Mad Lib:** `[Z character] just accomplished [X dream outcome] under [Y relatable conditions]`

Use a third party as proof — their relatable backstory makes the outcome feel achievable.

### Examples

**Example 1 — Creator economy:**
> "This creator, Lucas, just sold 10 million worth of products with his iPhone. Let me explain. Hi, I'm Lucas and this is creator marketing. So, how do you go from a broke college student to a creator founder with over 500,000 followers?"

- Dream outcome: $10M in revenue from a clothing brand
- Character: Lucas — broke college student → successful entrepreneur (relatable origin)
- Constraint-free: "just off his iPhone" → no fancy equipment needed
- Result: Estimated 2,000-5,000 leads if lead magnet was attached

**Example 2 — Virality/content:**
> "This creator posted the same hook nine times and went viral every single time."

- Dream outcome: Going viral at will, predictably
- Character: Unnamed creator — shown with low-effort looking videos (relatable)
- Constraint-free: "the same hook" → just repeat one formula
- Result: 2,000 leads

## Quick Selection Guide

| Situation | Recommended Template |
|-----------|---------------------|
| You have personal results to share | **About Me** |
| You want to share strategy/method | **If I** |
| Broadest possible audience appeal | **To You** |
| You want engagement + leads | **Can You** |
| You have a client/case study | **He/She Just Did** |
| You don't want to be on camera | **He/She Just Did** |
| You're a beginner creator | **To You** or **Can You** |

## Template Mixing

For maximum impact, create multiple hooks for the same content using different templates. Test which resonates with your audience. All 5 templates can be applied to any niche — the examples above are from social media/business but the framework works for fitness, finance, health, education, etc.
