# Desire-Based Hook Writer

Write hooks that turn views into leads and sales using desire-based framing — the highest-converting hook format across millions of tracked videos.

## Core Psychology

Desire-based hooks work by combining two elements in the first 1-2 lines:

1. **Dream Outcome** — The result the viewer wants (NOT the problem they have)
2. **Relatable Character** — Someone the viewer sees themselves in, with NO unfair advantages or conditions

**Why this converts**: Potential buyers have unsolved problems. When they hear a dream outcome from a relatable person, their subconscious locks on. If the character feels achievable (no crazy conditions), they're hooked 100% and want the method.

**Key insight**: Painting the dream outcome hooks MORE than stating the problem directly. It's one step removed — the viewer completes the puzzle in their mind and imagines what it would be like.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Information

Ask for missing info:

1. **Niche/product** — What are you selling or promoting?
2. **Target audience** — Who is the viewer? What's their pain?
3. **Dream outcome** — What result does the audience want most?
4. **Method/solution** — What's the simple approach? (Must feel easy/accessible)
5. **Platform** — TikTok, Reels, Shorts, YouTube?

### Step 2: Select Templates

Choose from 5 templates based on the creator's situation. See [references/templates.md](references/templates.md) for all templates with examples.

| Template | Character | Direction | Best For |
|----------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **About Me** | Creator | Looking back | Personal results, case studies |
| **If I** | Creator | Looking forward | Hypothetical replays, strategy content |
| **To You** | Viewer | Statement | Universal tips, broad audience |
| **Can You** | Viewer | Question | Challenge format, engagement bait |
| **He/She Just Did** | Third party | Case study | External proof, storytelling |

### Step 3: Write Hooks

For each hook, apply these rules:

- **Line 1-2 only** — Hook must land in the first 1-2 sentences
- **Dream outcome first** — Lead with what they WANT, not what they lack
- **Constraint-free method** — Make the solution feel easy: "one simple trick", "just three steps", "without doing anything crazy"
- **Relatable character** — Creator should seem normal, not intimidating. Third parties should feel like "someone like me"
- **No conditions** — Avoid price tags, special access, rare circumstances in the hook

### Step 4: Output Format

For each hook, provide:

```
### [Template Name] — [Vietnamese/English]

**Hook:**
> [1-2 sentence hook]

**Dream Outcome:** [What desire it triggers]
**Character Framing:** [Why the character feels relatable]
**Constraint-Free Signal:** [What makes it feel achievable]
```

## Mandatory Rules

- Write **3-5 hook variations** using different templates
- Each hook must contain BOTH dream outcome AND relatable character
- NEVER state the problem directly — always paint the desired result
- NEVER include conditions, high prices, or special circumstances in the hook
- Method must feel simple: "one thing", "three steps", "without doing anything crazy"
- If writing in Vietnamese, use conversational tone ("tui", "mình", natural speech)

## Pre-Completion Checklist

- [ ] Dream outcome clearly painted (not the problem)?
- [ ] Character is relatable with no unfair advantages?
- [ ] Method feels constraint-free and achievable?
- [ ] Hook lands in 1-2 sentences?
- [ ] Multiple template variations provided?
- [ ] Language matches platform (casual, natural)?
