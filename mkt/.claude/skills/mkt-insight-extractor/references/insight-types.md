# Insight Types Reference

## Purpose

Defines the 5 shapes an insight takes in a transcript.
Used by the insight-extractor skill to categorize and label each insight correctly.

## The 5 Types

### 1. Framework

**What it is:** A repeatable, teachable system the viewer/client can apply.

**Signs in transcript:**
- Step-by-step explanation
- Numbered process
- "First you do X, then Y"
- Clear sequence or structured method

**Example BUT:**
Most business owners jump straight to content — but they skip the extraction step that turns calls into assets.

> Framework insights sound like: "This is the process." / "Here's how it works." / "Do this, then this."
> They create **structure**.

### 2. Paradigm Shift

**What it is:** A moment where the mental model flips. They believed one thing; now they believe the opposite.

**Signs in transcript:**
- "Oh, I never thought of it that way."
- Sudden reframe
- Visible cognitive shift
- Repeating the insight back in different words

**Example BUT:**
Most people think more content solves the attention problem — but the real problem is they're producing content that has no mechanism to convert.

> Paradigm shifts **rewire belief systems**. They don't add information — they change perspective.

### 3. Warning

**What it is:** A mistake the viewer/client is about to make — or is already making — that will cost them.

**Signs in transcript:**
- "Don't do X."
- "I've seen this kill businesses."
- Recognizes a recurring trap
- Strong cautionary language

**Example BUT:**
Most founders automate the wrong things first — but automating output before fixing the thinking just produces bad content faster.

> Warnings **prevent pain**. They highlight landmines before the client steps on them.

### 4. Diagnosis

**What it is:** Identifying the root cause behind a symptom.

**Signs in transcript:**
- Describes a symptom ("I'm not getting leads.")
- Traces it to the real cause ("Because your offer isn't specific enough.")
- Moving from surface problem to underlying constraint

**Example BUT:**
Most business owners think they have a traffic problem — but the real bottleneck is a conversion problem disguised as reach.

> Diagnosis shifts focus from **symptom to system flaw**. It answers: "What's actually going on?"

### 5. Principle

**What it is:** A universal truth that governs a category of decisions. Not specific to one situation.

**Signs in transcript:**
- "This is always true."
- "The rule is..."
- Timeless framing
- Applies beyond this specific context

**Example BUT:**
You don't rise to the level of your goals — but you fall to the level of your systems.

> Principles are **durable**. They travel across industries, situations, and time. They guide judgment.

## Summary Table

| Type | Core Function | What It Does |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| Framework | Provides structure | Gives a repeatable system |
| Paradigm Shift | Changes belief | Flips mental model |
| Warning | Prevents damage | Highlights costly mistake |
| Diagnosis | Reveals root cause | Explains what's actually wrong |
| Principle | Guides judgment | States universal truth |
